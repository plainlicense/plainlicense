# Basic Start to a Style Guide

These are some basic style choices I made for the site. They are not exhaustive, but I wanted to document them so we could be consistent. Maybe one day we can have a more complete style guide. We do have the [plain writing guide][plain-writing-guide] and the [guide to the Plain License 'voice'][brand-guide] that cover some of these topics.

# Word Choices

| **Use**        | **Don't** Use  |
|:---------------|:---------------|
| :material-check: **lawyer** [^1] | :octicons-x-16: "attorney," "barrister", "solicitor" |
| :material-check: **change** |  :octicons-x-16: "alter", "modify", "amend", "revise" |
| :material-check: **copy**   | :octicons-x-16: "reproduce", "replicate" |
| :material-check: **give**   | :octicons-x-16: "convey", "furnish", "grant" |
| :material-check: **use**    | :octicons-x-16: "exploit", "utilize" |
| :material-check: **share** [^2] | :octicons-x-16: "distribute", "convey" |
| :material-check: **mix,** remix    | :octicons-x-16: "combine", "combination," "reuse," "adapt" |
| :material-check: [the] **work**   | :octicons-x-16: "software," "code", "product" |
| :material-check: **rules**, terms [^3] | :octicons-x-16: "permissions," "conditions," "limitations" |

Nothing here is permanent. I think there are good discussions to have about some of these choices. I just wanted to document them so we can be consistent in our writing. If you have a suggestion for a change, please open an issue or PR.

[^1]: Lawyer is a common word across the english-speaking world. It's the "plainer" form of the more formal, and more country-specific terms like "attorney," "barrister," or "solicitor." It's also the word a non-native speaker is most likely to know, and most resembles the word in other countries (abogado,)

[^2]: From a plain language perspective, share is clearly the more common word and more widely understood. I did struggle with this one, however. "Distribute" has a specific legal meaning and, while not truly *plain*, is still widely understood. It's a common 4th grade vocabulary word. Share is a more common word, and it is the word that most people would use to describe what they do with the work. It is also the word that most people would use to describe what they want to do with the work. So, I think it is a better choice for our licenses. You also have the influence of Creative Commons', which uses "share" in all of their licenses (not the super difficult legalese ["legal code"][legal-code], but the more accessible ["deed"][deed] that describes it). Generally, I followed their lead, as I also use "mix" or "remix" instead of "combine" or "combination".

[^3]: `Rules` works better than "terms" or "conditions" most of the time. Terms is OK as a way to talk about to the whole license, but it is not as clear as "rules" for the individual rules (conditions, limitations, permissions) that make up the license. While words like "conditions", and "limitations" have a legal meaning, from a reader's perspective, all of them are rules that they must follow. I think those differences also don't *really* matter when it comes to understanding the legal intent of the license. :tomato: :duck: :tomato:
