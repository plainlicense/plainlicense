# Plain License Voice and Messaging Guidelines

Plain License has a unique 'brand voice' that supports our mission to make legal documents clear and accessible for everyone. The voice is friendly, approachable, and clear, and it helps reassure people that they can understand legal concepts without needing a law degree.

This voice is used in all our communications, including our website, documentation, and social media. It reflects our commitment to transparency and accessibility.

## Key Characteristics of the Plain License 'Brand'

### Plain License's Identity

- **Rebellious yet Professional**: We challenge legal complexity while staying credible and trustworthy
- **Empowering**: We give people control through understanding, and help them feel **confident to create**
- **Clear**: We value direct, simple communication
- **Inclusive**: We write for everyone, regardless of legal background or language proficiency, and we avoid jargon and idioms
- **Community-Focused**: We're building a movement for clarity, not just a set of documents

### Plain License's Tone

- **Friendly**: We use a conversational tone, as if we're talking to a friend
- **Approachable**: We avoid legal jargon and complex terms, making it easy for anyone to understand
- **Supportive**: We encourage people to ask questions and seek help
- **Confident**: We present information clearly and with authority
- **Empathetic**: We understand that legal documents are hard for everyone to understand, and we want to help
- **Transparent**: We are open about our processes and decisions, and we want to help people understand them

## Motto

**Plain License: Freeing you to create.**

The motto captures our mission to empower creators by making legal documents clear and accessible. It reflects our commitment to transparency and inclusivity, and it encourages people to take control of their creative work.

### Supporting Messages

- "Create without fear"
- "Understand your rights"
- "Empower your creativity"
- "Clear words, real power" or "plain words, real power"
- "Your rights in plain language"
- "License to understand"

## Talking About Plain License

When sharing or talking about Plain License with others:

1. Focus on empowerment through understanding
2. Emphasize we're making licenses clearer, not replacing them
3. Highlight the practical benefits: confidence, creativity, sharing
4. Emphasize our community and collaborative spirit
5. Use inclusive language that welcomes everyone

### We Are Open Source, But We're Not *About* Open Source

Plain License *is* an open source project, but we are not an open source *advocacy* project.

People often think of creative licenses as the same as 'open source' or 'free software'. There are good reasons for that. The open source community led important innovations with [permissive][permissive] and [copyleft][copyleft] creative licenses that make people assume that relationship. Most creative licenses *are* open source.

Plain License supports *any* creative work, and we want to support creators *any way they choose to share their work*. Our goal is *not sharing*, it's *creation* -- we think all people deserve to understand the creator's intent. Unfortunately, creators sometimes share work without understanding the terms, and we want to help them choose a license that works for them and their goals.

Plain License itself *is* an open source project, dedicated to the public [under our own version of the Unlicense, the Plain Unlicense][plain_unlicense].

## Describing Your Use of Plain License

When describing your use of Plain License, here are some ways to talk about it:

- "My work uses the Plain MIT License, so you can clearly understand your rights and how you can use it."
- "I use Plain License to make sure everyone knows what they can do with my work."

## Communication Examples

### Strong Examples

✅ "Plain License transforms legal documents so everyone can understand them"
✅ "You have the right to understand your legal agreements"
✅ "Clear licenses lead to more creation and sharing"
✅ "Make your terms easy for everyone to understand"

### Avoid

❌ "Our licenses are better than traditional licenses"
❌ "Legal language is bad"
❌ "You must use plain language"
❌ "Only lawyers understand traditional licenses"

## Visual Identity

-   **Logo**. Our logo is a simple abstract design representing clarity and accessibility. It speaks to reducing complexity. Use it consistently across all platforms. Never change the logo or use it in a way that misrepresents our mission. It should always appear alone with enough space around it. If it's crowded by other logos or graphics, it loses its core message of clarity and simplicity.
-   **Color Palette**. Use our color palette to create a consistent look across all communications. Use the colors in the same way in all places and in a way that reflects our community 'brand' identity.
    - Primary/Logo Color: #15db95 A vibrant teal green that represents clarity and rebellion
    - Secondary Colors: #0d19a3 a vibrant blue, and #e4c580 a muted gold
    - Supporting Colors: #f4e4c1 a lighter gold, and #6ca6c1 a grayish sky blue
-   **Typography**. Always use clean, sans-serif fonts for readability. Our preferred fonts (all free [Google fonts][googlefonts]) are:
    - 'Plain License' wordmark: Raleway.
    - Headings and Body Text: Inter
    - Monospace and Code: Source Code Pro
    - Impactful Text: Bangers Regular. Use this *very* sparingly. It's great for rebellious headlines in very large sizes, but not for body text or headings.
-   **Imagery**. Use images that reflect our mission of clarity and accessibility or support our rebellious, anti-establishment tone. Avoid overly complex or abstract images that may confuse our message. Examples:
    - Images that contrast establishment (complexity) with freedom (clarity)
    - Urban/street art that suggests rebellion and creativity
    - Visual metaphors of breaking free, escaping confusion, or opening doors

## Remember

Plain language isn't about making things simple. It's about making them clear. Our goal isn't to dumb things down, but to make difficult things understandable.
